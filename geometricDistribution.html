Geometric Distribution

Statistics

In a geometric distribution, the probability of the first success on the x-th trial is given by the formula:

P(X = x) = (1 - p)^(x - 1) * p

Example: This could represent the number of coin flips needed to get the first heads, where each flip has a probability "p" of landing heads.
