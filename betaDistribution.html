Beta Distribution

Statistics

The Beta distribution is a family of continuous probability distributions defined on the interval [0, 1], and parameterized by two positive shape parameters, "α" (alpha) and "β" (beta). The formula is given by:

f(x; α, β) = x^(α-1) * (1-x)^(β-1) / B(α, β)

Example: The Beta distribution is commonly used to model the behavior of random variables limited to intervals of finite length in a variety of disciplines.
