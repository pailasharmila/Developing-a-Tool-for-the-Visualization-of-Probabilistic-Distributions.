Normal Distribution

Statistics

For a random variable x, with mean "μ" and standard deviation "σ", the normal distribution formula is given by:

f(x) = (1/√(2πσ²)) * exp[-(x-μ)² / 2σ²]

Example: This could represent heights of people, where most people are near the average height (mean), and fewer are much taller or shorter (extreme values).
